Understanding Wildfires

Wildfires are fast-spreading fires fueled by dry vegetation and wind. They can start from natural causes like lightning or human actions like unattended campfires. California experiences thousands of wildfires yearly, leading to evacuations, health risks, and massive damage. Being prepared is not optional.This is a quick map of how fast the Palisades spread, as you see it covered a large area in little to no time. Being prepared is very important.

What To Do During a Wildfire

  • Evacuate immediately if authorities tell you to.
  • Cover your nose and mouth with an N95 mask.
  • Use fire blankets if you're trapped or near flames.
  • Have a flashlight ready if power goes out.
  • Keep a go-bag packed and near the door.
